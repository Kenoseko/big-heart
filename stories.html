5 Best Weekend Activities for Kids in Nairobi (That Don't Involve Screens!)

📅 Published: May 2026 • 👤 By: Big Heart Kenya Team

Finding wholesome, engaging weekend activities for kids in Nairobi can sometimes feel like a puzzle. If you are trying to cut down on TV time and want your children to experience real, hands-on fun, Nairobi has some amazing hidden gems!

1. Interactive Storytelling Circles

Reading doesn't have to feel like schoolwork! Interactive storytelling sessions, like our weekend StoryLab classes at Big Heart, bring books to life with music, drawing, and theatrical expressions. It's one of the most popular children activities in Kenya for boosting vocabulary and social confidence early on.

2. Outdoor Nature Trails & Sports

When looking for kids fun places in Nairobi, nature is your best friend. A family Saturday spent cycling or walking through Karura Forest gives children fresh air and heavy physical movement. Pairing nature with structured games like our ActiveHub sports sessions teaches them vital teamwork and sportsmanship skills away from a digital display.

3. Creative Crafting and LEGO Building Workshops

Instead of buying new toys, let them build their own! Creative workshops allow kids to build prototypes, sculpt with clay, or construct massive forts with loose parts. This type of play sharpens problem-solving, cognitive abilities, and spatial awareness.

How to Pick the Best Activity This Weekend

The secret is balance. Mix a bit of heavy physical exercise with quiet, imaginative reading or making sessions. Want a structured space where your child can experience all of this in one secure place? Check out our upcoming weekend sessions right here on our platform!

Why Creative Play is the Secret Weapon for Success in Kenya's CBC Curriculum

📅 Published: May 2026 • 👤 By: Teacher Aisha Njoroge

The CBC curriculum in Kenya has changed the way we look at education. Moving away from traditional rote memorization, the Competency Based Curriculum Kenya places immense focus on practical learning, critical thinking, values, and imagination. But did you know that the best way to support your child's learning journey at home isn't through extra homework sheets? It's through creative play!

Building Core Competencies Outside the Classroom

The CBC Framework is built on key core pillars: Communication, Collaboration, Critical Thinking, and Creativity. When kids engage in unstructured creative play, they are practicing these exact skills naturally:

  • Communication: Role-playing during storytelling or puppet shows forces kids to articulate thoughts clearly.
  • Collaboration: Building a fort out of cardboard box materials with other children requires negotiations and teamwork.
  • Critical Thinking: Figuring out why a tower keeps falling over stretches engineering logic.

Bringing Play-Based Learning Home

Parents can easily nurture childhood development in Kenya by dedicating just an hour a day to sensory exploration or open-ended building tasks. At Big Heart, our PlayLab and Builders programs are designed to perfectly match these educational goals, ensuring your child is learning without even realizing it because they are having so much fun!

Top Children's Books by African Authors to Read with Your Kids Today

📅 Published: June 2026 • 👤 By: Big Heart Reading Club

To cultivate a lifelong reading culture in Kenya, children need to read tales where they can see their own lives, backgrounds, names, and neighborhoods reflected beautifully on the page. Finding great children's books by African authors transforms reading time from a standard chore into an exciting window of self-discovery.

Why Representation Matters in Children's Literacy

When young kids read about characters going to local markets, celebrating traditional holidays, or exploring landscapes like the East African savannah, a deep connection sparks. It makes the world of literature feel close and accessible, rather than foreign or remote. This familiarity dramatically increases reading comprehension and developmental confidence.

Our Top Recommended Storybooks for Kids in Kenya

From whimsical folk tales with important moral compass highlights to modern city adventures set right here in Nairobi, there are brilliant options to pick from. Look out for locally published gems that feature vivid illustrations and positive, community-minded themes.

How We Grow Eager Readers

If your child is struggling to build an independent routine, try reading aloud together every evening, or join a vibrant weekly circle like our ReadCircle club. Surrounded by active peers and passionate facilitators, children learn to voice text accurately, swap favorites, and build fluency together smoothly.
